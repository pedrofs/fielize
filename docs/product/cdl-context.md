# CDL Context — How Brazilian merchant associations work

> Context primer for anyone building, designing, or selling into the Brazilian CDL ecosystem. None of the platform decisions make sense without this.

---

## What is a CDL

**CDL = Câmara de Dirigentes Lojistas.** A local merchant association — a non-profit civic entity that represents the retail sector at the municipal level. Members are local shop owners (associados) who pay a monthly fee and receive a bundle of services in return.

CDLs exist in nearly every Brazilian municipality with a meaningful retail base. There are approximately **2,000 of them**.

---

## Three-tier federation system (Sistema CNDL)

```
CNDL (Confederação Nacional dos Dirigentes Lojistas)
   │
   ├── FCDL/RS  (Federação dos CDLs do estado)  ──── one per state, 27 total
   │     │
   │     ├── CDL Jaguarão     ─── local
   │     ├── CDL Pelotas      ─── local
   │     ├── CDL Bagé         ─── local
   │     └── ...              ─── ~150 more local CDLs in RS alone
   │
   ├── FCDL/SC
   ├── FCDL/MG
   └── ... 24 more state federations
```

- **CNDL** (national) — political advocacy, sets standards, owns SPC Brasil infrastructure.
- **FCDL** (state) — coordinates local CDLs, runs shared services, mediates between national and local.
- **CDL** (local) — autonomous; sets own bylaws, fees, campaigns. Volunteer-led with one paid staffer running day-to-day.

---

## What associates pay for (the historical bundle)

| Service | Notes |
|---|---|
| **SPC Brasil consultations** | The historical anchor. Members can query the database (largest credit-information database in Latin America: 180M+ individuals, 26M+ companies, 50M+ queries/month) and report defaulters. |
| **Cobrança service** | CDL sends formal collection letters via Correios to defaulting customers. ~55% recovery in 30 days, ~89% by day 120 per CNDL marketing. |
| **Convênios** | Discounts with health plans, accountants, lawyers, training providers. |
| **Networking events** | Almoços, palestras, formal dinners. |
| **Training** | Often via SEBRAE partnerships. |
| **Lobby / representation** | Talks to prefeitura, vereadores, state legislature on behalf of local commerce. |
| **Public-facing campaigns** | Liquida, Black Friday coordination, Mother's Day raffles, Dia Livre de Impostos, Christmas illumination. |

## Fees in small interior cities

For a CDL the size of CDL Jaguarão (~50 merchants):

- **Monthly mensalidade:** R$80–300 per associate, tiered by service plan
- **Adesão (one-time):** R$100–500
- **Operating budget:** ~R$8–15k/month total (mostly volunteer labor, often one paid secretária or diretor executivo)

For larger CDLs in capitals, fees can be 5–10× higher and operating budgets are commensurate.

## Internal political structure

Local CDL governance:

- **Diretoria Executiva** — elected by the assembleia geral, typically 2 or 3-year mandate. One reelection allowed by most bylaws. The current CDL Jaguarão gestão is "2025–2027" (3-year term).
- **Presidente** — political head. Public face. Usually a respected local entrepreneur. Does not necessarily run day-to-day operations.
- **Diretor Executivo / Secretária(o) Executivo(a)** — paid staff, often the actual operator. Outlasts presidents. Frequently the real decision-maker on tactical matters. **This is often who you sell to** — the president gives political approval, but the operator chooses tools.
- **Conselho Deliberativo** — sets direction, meets monthly or quarterly.
- **Conselho Fiscal** — oversight.
- **Conselho Consultivo** (sometimes) — former presidents, advisors.

**CDL Jovem** is a parallel youth wing — under-40 lojistas. Often the political pipeline (today's CDL Jovem president = tomorrow's CDL president). Frequently the more tech-forward, change-friendly group. Worth identifying when entering a new CDL.

## Campaigns CDLs typically run

Every one of these is a candidate template for the platform:

- **Pasaporte de Compras / fronteira campaigns** (border cities) — tourist-facing
- **Datas comerciais** — Mother's Day, Father's Day, Children's Day, Christmas, with lottery / cashback hooks
- **Liquida** — coordinated end-of-season sales
- **Black Friday** — local edition
- **Dia Livre de Impostos** — political advocacy event with high media visibility
- **Lojista do Ano** awards
- **Iluminação de Natal** coordination
- **Campanha do Dia das Mães / Dia dos Namorados / Dia das Crianças** with raffles
- **Volta às Aulas** seasonal campaigns

## Strategic backdrop: SPC Brasil commoditization

This is the single most important strategic frame to carry into every conversation with CDL leadership.

**What's happening:**
- Serasa, Boa Vista, and Quod offer competing credit databases.
- Major banks (Bradesco, Itaú, Nubank) have first-party credit data and are becoming credit-information distributors.
- Fintechs offer API-based credit checks at lower price points and with better integration.
- Big Tech (Meta, Google) have first-party purchase intent and behavioral data that supplements traditional credit info.

**The result:** SPC is no longer a scarce resource. The historical reason to be associated to a CDL is eroding.

**What this means for our pitch:**

When selling to a CDL, the question is not "do you want a campaign tool?" — it's *"what is your value to associates in 5 years?"* Phrased that way, a campaign platform stops being a nice-to-have promotional tool and becomes a candidate replacement value driver.

**Don't say "SPC is dying"** — that's tactless and the leadership knows it better than you do. Let your framing imply the question. They'll fill in the answer themselves.

## Political dynamics to surface in discovery

When approaching a new CDL, ask about (or at least be aware of):

- **The diretor executivo** — who they are, how long they've been there. Often the real long-term decision-maker.
- **Last election** — was the chapa contested or consensus? Contested means there are factions; consensus means tighter clique.
- **ACI relationship** — many cities have a parallel **Associação Comercial e Industrial** that's sometimes complementary, sometimes turf-rival.
- **Prefeitura relationship** — particularly relevant in border cities where Free Shop / fronteira commerce policy involves the city government.
- **CDL Jovem presence** — exists in this CDL? Active? Tech-savvy?
- **Existing tech vendor** — already have a software partner for digital initiatives? They're your political risk surface.
